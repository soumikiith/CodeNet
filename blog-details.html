Shedding light on the Advanced Aspects of Computer Science


Preamble


Soumik Kumar Basu  |  06.05.2023 16:45

Welcome to our first blog, Preamble. We will try to cover the following points in this blog one by one: (1) What will our future blogs cover? (2) Why have we chosen to write blogs? (3) For whom will the blogs be beneficial?

A prompt answer to the first question can be 'Basic and Advanced topics of Computer Science.' However, Computer Science is itself a vast domain. Covering each and every area would be nearly impossible. So, the main focus of our blogs will be those topics that don't get the spotlight very often. The broad areas that we plan to cover are: (1) Compiler Analysis and Implementation (2) Approximation Algorithms (3) Parameterized Algorithms (4) Graph Theory (5) Software-defined Networks (6) Network Programming (7) High Performance Computing (8) NLP (9) Quantum Computing and many more. We believe that some of you have encountered these topics before. However, when you tried to learn more about them, you may have stopped because the content was boring or resources were unavailable. Honestly speaking, we were also dissatisfied when we started our research. Every day, we used to wonder why there couldn't be more interesting documentation. We had a difficult start, and our journey hasn't been easy so far. We also are assured, that it will continue to be challenging. However, we have a vision now to make good documentation about the topics we have studied till now. We believe that these documents will make things easier for future researchers and developers. Lastly, our blogs are for people who love learning and researching these topics. We want to help them understand these topics easily. We hope our blogs will be useful for students, researchers, and developers working actively in the field of 'Computer Science & Engineering.' In the future, we will accept new blogs from people in different fields to make our project more successful.

“The purpose of learning is growth, and our minds, unlike our bodies, can continue growing as we continue to live.” —Mortimer Adler
